10/13/2023

Book review: The Confidence Game by Maria Konnikova

I enjoyed this a lot! It was very interesting to learn about some of the common logical fallacies people fall into in daily life, and how they can be exploited. I do feel like the delineation between steps of the con got muddled sometimes, and that the examples chosen weren't exact one-to-one demonstrations of those steps. However, it was especially enlightening reading it while playing as and with changelings in a D&D campaign. Maybe that's not the intended use of this book. Anyways, I recommend it for anyone interested in the topic, people who have fallen victim to scams and cons (the book emphasizes frequently that it's rarely your fault), and people who have never or think they'd never get caught up in something like that. I already knew the disclaimer that even smart people can be suckered, but never before have I noticed the flaws in my own mentality so keenly. I'm not sure if I'll be able to consciously correct them, but it was a great read!

09/15/2023

Book review: Tao Te Ching by Lao Tzu, translated by Ursula K. Le Guin

While I'm disappointed that I wasn't able to find a copy of the translation I was looking for, I enjoyed Le Guin's commentary! However, I tried to not read too much into it and come up with my own interpretations of the text. I jumped around in the text based on what themes I thought were relevant for the day. Of course, the Taoteching still has a lot of insight worth pondering over in the modern age. I'd recommend it, if only to have in mind for how society functions. It's a lot about going with the flow and accepting things as they are, which is good to think about during stressful times in life. While not applicable to everything, I do think my state of mind has improved having read it. As for which translation is best, I'd have to read more translations!

07/21/2023

Book review: Gory Details by Erika Engelhaupt

I enjoyed this book a lot! As I've gotten older and more busy I appreciate books with more episodic concepts that are easy to put down and pick back up, and the focus on a new story each time was engaging. I enjoyed the tieback of every story at the epilogue where the purpose of studying and cataloguing these discoveries was acknowledged as necessary for people to learn and have a healthier attitude towards the world around us and ourselves. Overall, highly recommend if you aren't squeamish and are interested in more bizarre scientific discoveries being made, or if you are squeamish and want to build your tolerance. There are no images and descriptions are kept fairly brief, but there are more detailed drawings to section off the book.
